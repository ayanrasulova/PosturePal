Exercises to Improve Posture

Incorporate these exercises into your routine to strengthen muscles and improve alignment:

  • Plank: Builds core strength for a neutral spine [4].
  • Bird-Dog: Enhances spinal alignment and core stability [4].
  • Superman Pose: Strengthens back muscles and glutes [5].
  • Seated Band Pull-Apart: Improves shoulder alignment [4].
  • Single-leg Glute Bridge: Promotes hip stability and upright posture [4].

Articles & Research

  • Why Good Posture Matters (Harvard Health): Explains the benefits of good posture for balance, injury prevention, and overall health [6].
  • The Importance of Good Posture for Students: Highlights how poor posture affects focus, energy levels, and long-term well-being in students [1].
  • Computer Usage and Musculoskeletal Discomfort: A study on how extended computer use impacts college students’ posture and health [2].

Posture Tips for College Students

  • Take breaks every 30 minutes when using a computer or studying.
  • Use ergonomic furniture to support your back.
  • Avoid slouching while looking at screens—keep your chin parallel to the floor.
  • Engage in regular physical activity like yoga or Pilates to strengthen core muscles [3] [6].

By following these resources, you can build healthier habits that will benefit you both now and in the future!
