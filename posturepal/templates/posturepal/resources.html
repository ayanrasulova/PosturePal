Posture Resources

Exercises and Stretches

Incorporate these exercises and stretches into your routine to improve posture and relieve tension:

  • Chin Tucks: Strengthen neck and upper back muscles by gently tucking your chin towards your chest while sitting upright. Hold for 2-3 seconds; repeat 15 times for 2 sets.
  • Wall Angels: Stand against a wall, raise your arms to shoulder height, and move them up and down while keeping contact with the wall. This strengthens upper back and shoulder muscles.
  • Cat-Cow Stretch: Alternate between arching and rounding your back on hands and knees to increase spinal flexibility.
  • Chest Stretch: Interlace your fingers behind your back, straighten your arms, and lift them away from your body. Hold for 30 seconds to counteract forward-rounded shoulders.
  • Hip Flexor Stretch: Release tension in the hips by lunging forward with one leg while keeping the other knee on the ground. Hold for 30 seconds per side.

Posture Tips

  • Adjust Your Backpack: Keep it close to your back and distribute weight evenly using both straps to reduce spinal stress.
  • Rearrange Your Desk: Ensure screens are at eye level, supplies are within reach, and avoid slouching while studying.
  • Follow the 90-90-90 Rule: Keep knees, hips, and elbows at 90-degree angles when seated. Feet should be flat on the floor, with a desk height that supports this alignment.
  • Take Breaks: Stand up or stretch every 30 minutes to avoid prolonged strain on your spine.

  • The Effect of Sitting Posture on Cognitive Performance: Shows improved attention and memory with correct posture (published in Ergonomics).
  • Posture and How It Affects Your Health: Discusses how poor posture leads to imbalances, pain, and reduced breathing efficiency.

Ergonomic Guidelines

Set up an ergonomic workspace to support better posture:

  • Height-Adjustable Furniture: Use desks and chairs that allow customization for proper alignment of knees, hips, and elbows at 90 degrees.
  • Screen Placement: Position monitors so you look slightly above the screen without tilting your head downward.
  • Core Engagement While Standing: Practice standing with a straight spine, relaxed shoulders, and engaged core muscles to maintain balance.
  • Tiltable Desktops: These can help reduce neck strain by improving the angle of study materials or screens.

By following these exercises, tips, research insights, and ergonomic guidelines, you can build sustainable habits for better posture!
